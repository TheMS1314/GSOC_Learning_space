# Motivation

## Who I am
I'm a third-year B.Tech student at Nirma University in Ahmedabad, India.
I'm solid with Python and have been working on ML projects for a while now,
currently getting into deep learning as well. I tend to think about problems
from scratch — like if I see something happening around me I naturally start
wondering if and how it could be modelled or solved computationally.

## Why Mesa
## Why Mesa
I'm someone who genuinely enjoys thinking about how the world works —
I have long conversations with friends about philosophy, society, human
behavior, why people do what they do. It's just how my mind works in
free time.

So one day I was thinking about something that bothers me philosophically —
how people publicly enforce rules and norms they privately hate, just
because everyone else seems to be doing it. Nobody actually believes in
it but the whole society acts like everyone does. I kept wondering if
that could actually be modelled mathematically — like under what conditions
does that trap form and what finally breaks it?

That thinking led me to agent-based modelling and then to Mesa. When I
found the Emperor's Dilemma example it had already formalized exactly
what I was sitting with. That's what made me stay.

The Continuous Space side pulled me in for a different reason — the idea
that flocking emerges from just three simple local rules with no central
coordination connects to the same philosophical question. How does
complex collective behavior emerge from individuals who have no idea
what the whole is doing?

## What I want to learn
I want to get better at contributing to something bigger than my own projects.
Until now most of my code has been solo — ML experiments, personal projects.
GSoC is me deliberately stepping into a collaborative codebase and learning
how that actually works. How do you write code that fits someone else's
architecture? How do you discuss a problem before jumping to a solution?
How do you make something maintainable, not just functional? That's what
I'm here to figure out.

## Where I want to go
Long term I want to work on problems where AI intersects with real social or
scientific questions. Mesa feels like a natural place to build the kind of
thinking that leads there.As well miuch more interestred about how AGI would impact society and all.
